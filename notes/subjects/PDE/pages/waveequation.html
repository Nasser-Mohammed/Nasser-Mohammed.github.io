Wave Equation

The wave equation arises naturally in physical systems such as vibrating strings and membranes. It is the archetype of a second-order hyperbolic PDE.

Derivation

For a string of length \(L\) with fixed ends, let \(u(x,t)\) represent the transverse displacement.

Consider a small element of string between \(x\) and \(x+\Delta x\). Tension \(T\) acts along the string. The vertical component of tension is

\[ T\sin\theta \approx T \frac{\partial u}{\partial x}. \]

Newton’s second law: force = mass × acceleration. The vertical force is

\[ \rho \Delta x \frac{\partial^2 u}{\partial t^2} = T \frac{\partial u}{\partial x}\Big|_{x+\Delta x} - T \frac{\partial u}{\partial x}\Big|_{x}. \]

Divide through by \(\Delta x\) and take the limit:

\[ \rho \frac{\partial^2 u}{\partial t^2} = T \frac{\partial^2 u}{\partial x^2}. \]

Let \(c^2 = T/\rho\). Then

\[ u_{tt} = c^2 u_{xx}. \]

Initial and Boundary Conditions

To solve, we need:

  • Initial displacement: \(u(x,0)=f(x)\)
  • Initial velocity: \(u_t(x,0)=g(x)\)
  • Boundary conditions (e.g., fixed ends): \(u(0,t)=u(L,t)=0\)

Separation of Variables

Assume \(u(x,t)=X(x)T(t)\). Substituting:

\[ \frac{T''}{c^2 T} = \frac{X''}{X} = -\lambda. \]

This yields two ODEs:

\[ X''+\lambda X=0, \quad T''+c^2\lambda T=0. \]

Spatial Equation

With boundary conditions \(X(0)=X(L)=0\):

\[ X_n(x)=\sin\left(\frac{n\pi x}{L}\right),\quad \lambda_n=\left(\frac{n\pi}{L}\right)^2. \]

Temporal Equation

Solutions are:

\[ T_n(t)=A_n\cos\left(\frac{n\pi c t}{L}\right) +B_n\sin\left(\frac{n\pi c t}{L}\right). \]

General Solution

\[ u(x,t)=\sum_{n=1}^\infty \left[A_n\cos\left(\frac{n\pi c t}{L}\right) +B_n\sin\left(\frac{n\pi c t}{L}\right)\right] \sin\left(\frac{n\pi x}{L}\right). \]

Coefficients \(A_n,B_n\) are determined from the initial conditions using Fourier sine series expansions.

d’Alembert’s Solution

On an infinite string, the solution takes the form:

\[ u(x,t)=\tfrac{1}{2}\big[f(x-ct)+f(x+ct)\big] +\tfrac{1}{2c}\int_{x-ct}^{x+ct} g(s)\,ds. \]

This represents the superposition of traveling waves moving left and right with speed \(c\).
